A field guide to five days on the road

Oregon

Portland to the sea, behind the waterfalls, up the mountain, around the deepest lake in America, and over the dunes. Three friends. One loop. July 20–25, 2026.

Ecola State Park — Wikimedia

Some trips are an escape. This one is a circle — drawn clockwise from Portland through everything Oregon does best: a bonfire on the sand at Cannon Beach, a trail that ducks behind ten waterfalls, two nights of hot-spring mineral water, the alpine quiet of Mount Hood, the impossible blue of Crater Lake, and one loud, fast morning on the dunes before the forest carries us home.

Morning

Wheels down at PDX, 9:45 a.m. Pick up the rental car and drive west through the Coast Range — ninety minutes from jet bridge to ocean air.

Afternoon

Check into the oceanfront room, find a fresh, savory lunch in town, then walk the shoreline to Haystack Rock — 235 feet of basalt standing in the surf, tide pools at its feet.

Evening

The resort staff builds the private beach bonfire; we supply the cold beer. First sunset of the trip, straight into the Pacific.

Morning

Check out and drive two hours inland to Silver Falls State Park — the state's largest, and its best-kept worst-kept secret.

Afternoon

The Trail of Ten Falls: a shaded canyon loop that passes ten waterfalls and walks you directly behind the tallest of them, where the curtain of water hangs a few feet from your face. Then push north into the Columbia River Gorge.

Evening

Check in, eat well, and lower seven miles of hiking into the mineral pools until it disappears.

Morning

Cool and quiet: fifteen minutes to Multnomah Falls and Latourell Falls before the crowds wake up. The classic shots, no strangers in them.

Afternoon

Ride the Gorge east, then climb south into thin, crisp air. From the stone terraces of the historic Timberline Lodge, half of Oregon lays out below and the summit hangs above.

Evening

Back down the mountain for a second consecutive night in the mineral water. Some routines are worth two nights.

Morning

Check out of the springs and point the car south. Road-trip morning: gas, coffee, a long playlist, and Oregon rolling past the windows.

Afternoon

Crater Lake National Park. Take Rim Drive slowly — every pull-out reframes a collapsed volcano holding the bluest water any of us will ever see.

Evening

Drive west and sleep next to the dunes. Tomorrow starts loud.

Morning

9:00 a.m., Oregon Dunes National Recreation Area. Two and a half hours of ATVs or a fast buggy tour over the sand peaks — cool air, firm sand, full throttle.

Afternoon

The slow drive: northeast into the Willamette National Forest. With the riding done there is no schedule — pull over for the roaring rivers, walk under the old-growth Douglas firs, take the long way on purpose.

Evening

Finish the loop at a hotel beside the airport. In the morning: coffee, gate, home.

Appendix

The small print that keeps five days smooth.

Pack

  • Layers — the coast runs cool, even in July
  • Real hiking shoes; the falls trail stays misty
  • Swimsuits for the mineral pools
  • Sunglasses and sunscreen for the dunes
  • A beanie for the bonfire

The Car

  • Pick up at PDX, Monday ~10 a.m.
  • Top off fuel before Crater Lake
  • Some Oregon stations pump for you — let them
  • Download offline maps; the passes drop signal

Book Ahead

  • Crater Lake entrance — $30 per car
  • ATV rental for Friday, 9 a.m.
  • Bonfire slot with the Surfsand staff
  • Timberline lunch, if a table matters
